Privacy Policy

Last updated: 08/06/2026

1. Introduction

Medivum is a personal health notebook designed for recording vaccinations, doctor visits, reminders, attachments, and other health-related notes.

User privacy is important to us. The app is designed as a local tool that stores data on the user's device.

2. What data the app stores

The app may store information entered by the user, including:

  • user profiles,
  • PIN code for app protection,
  • vaccination records,
  • vaccination doses and reminder dates,
  • doctor visit records,
  • doctor and specialty information,
  • notes and visit records,
  • photos, PDF documents, and other attachments,
  • app settings.

3. Where data is stored

All data is stored locally on the user's device.

The app does not normally send personal or health data to a remote server. The app operator does not have access to data stored on the user's device.

4. User profiles and PIN

On first launch, the app may require the user to create a personal profile and set a PIN code.

This is not an online registration and does not create a server-side account. The profile and PIN are used only for local app usage and protection on the user's device.

5. App permissions

The app may request certain device permissions.

Camera

Used to take photos that the user may attach to health records.

Photos and Files

Used to select and store attachments, such as photos or PDF documents.

Notifications

Used to display local reminders configured by the user.

6. Data Sharing

The app does not share personal or health data with third parties.

The app does not contain advertising and does not use analytics tools to track users.

7. Security

The app can be protected with a PIN code. We also recommend using device-level security, such as a system PIN, password, or biometric authentication.

The user is responsible for securing their device and protecting access to the app.

8. Attachments and Local Files

Attachments added to the app may be copied into the app's local storage. This allows them to remain available in the app even if the user deletes the original file or photo from another location on the device.

9. User Control

Because data is stored locally on the device, the user remains in control of their data.

The user can delete data by removing individual records, deleting attachments, or uninstalling the app.

10. GDPR

The app is designed so that personal and health data remains locally on the user's device and is not normally transferred to the app operator.

If features such as cloud synchronization or online backup are added in the future, this Privacy Policy will be updated.

11. Changes to this Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time. The current version will always be available on this website and within the app.
